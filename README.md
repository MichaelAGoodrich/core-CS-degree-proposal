# Computer Science BS Degree Proposal

This repository contains the complete documentation for a proposed restructuring of the Computer Science Bachelor of Science degree at BYU. The proposal shifts from a course-accumulation model to a **conceptual, abstraction-centered curriculum** designed for students entering a rapidly evolving computing profession with a terminal bachelor's degree.

## Overview

The proposed degree emphasizes **enduring CS abstractions** (algorithms, systems, data, AI, ethics) rather than short-lived tools, helping graduates remain adaptable over 10–15 years as technologies evolve. Notably, this proposal aligns closely with the 2025 Unit Review's findings concerning unclear core competencies and insufficient integration of theoretical and practical skills. Total degree hours: **74 credits**.

### Key Principles

- **Computer Science as the Science of Abstraction**: Focus on durable conceptual models over transient frameworks
- **Terminal BS with Professional Intent**: Designed for industry readiness, not as a pipeline to graduate research
- **Breadth and Depth**: Explicit requirements across four core areas plus advanced specialization
- **Required Experiential Learning**: Capstone, internship, or research experience

These principles validate recommendations from the 2025 Unit Review for clearer competencies, stronger industry preparation, and structured professional development.

### Key Constraint
- **Evolutionary (not Revolutionary) Roll Out**: We already have a very good degree program. Improve it while making fewest changes possible to existing classes and having the least impact on faculty. This approach aligns with the unit review's emphasis on strengthening the program while respecting the quality of existing coursework.

### Alignment with Unit Review

This proposal independently converges with findings from the 2025 Unit Review on three critical areas:
- **Clear Core Competencies**: Explicit definition through CS Foundations, Junior Core structure, and required breadth/depth areas
- **Theory-Practice Balance**: Curriculum balances foundational theory with required experiential learning (capstone, internship, or research)
- **Structured Professional Development**: Mandatory experiential component ensures all students engage in reflection and skill-building under faculty guidance

See [unit-review/](unit-review/) for detailed excerpts and alignment notes.

## Major Changes from Existing Degree

| Aspect | Change | Unit Review Alignment |
|--------|--------|----------------------|
| **Structure** | Split into CS Foundations → Junior Core → Breadth/Depth/Capstone | Establishes explicit core competencies |
| **AI Coverage** | Now **required** in junior core (previously elective) | Industry-relevant preparation |
| **Databases** | Now **required** in junior core (previously elective) | Industry-relevant preparation |
| **Breadth** | **Explicit requirement** across 4 areas (previously implicit) | Clear, intentional breadth structure |
| **Depth** | **Explicit 400-level requirement** (previously optional) | Specialization and depth reinforcement |
| **Experiential Learning** | **Required capstone/internship/research** (previously optional) | Structured professional development and reflection |
| **Math/Physics** | Physics removed; focus on **data-oriented analytics** | Industry-aligned skill development |
| **Philosophy** | Emphasis on **abstractions and paradigms** for 10–15 year adaptability | Enduring competencies for evolving field |
