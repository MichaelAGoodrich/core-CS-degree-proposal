# AI Course: Perspective from ACM Curriculum

## Overview

### From ACM Curriculum Document

> “The study of Artificial Intelligence prepares students to determine when an AI approach is appropriate for a given problem, identify appropriate representations and reasoning mechanisms, implement them, and evaluate them with respect to both performance and their broader societal impact.”

> “There are explicit goals to develop basic AI literacy and critical thinking in every computer science student, given the breadth of interconnections between AI and other knowledge areas in practice.”

---

## Proposed CS 370

### Course Goal

Build strong mental models of key AI paradigms — how they work, when they apply, what their limits are, and how to reason ethically about their use in real systems.

### Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

1. Explain and experiment with core AI paradigms using Jupyter notebooks and lab exercises: search, planning, probabilistic reasoning, optimization, and learning.
2. Compare and evaluate the strengths and limitations of different paradigms when solving a common problem.
3. Interpret visualizations and experimental results to refine hypotheses about algorithmic behavior.
4. Recognize the paradigms underlying real-world AI tools and explain how they interact in hybrid systems. *(Don’t know how to do this)*
5. Analyze ethical and sociotechnical issues (including bias, fairness, and explainability) by engaging with technical examples and popular sources.
