# CS 370: Artificial Intelligence

CS 370 is proposed as a required junior-core course because AI is now a foundational area of computer science, and every graduate should understand core AI concepts, paradigms, and limits.

## Required AI Course vs. AI Across the Curriculum

This required course establishes essential AI concepts for all students. It is distinct from the broader effort to integrate AI across multiple courses. For the cross-curricular integration plan, see [ai-across-curriculum/README.md](../../ai-across-curriculum/README.md).

## Why a New Course Instead of CS 270

CS 270 is a refactor and renumbering of the former 400-level Introduction to Machine Learning course and does not include many of the AI topics emphasized in the ACM guidelines. The committee therefore proposes CS 370 as a broader AI paradigms course that aligns with those guidelines. See [junior-core/370/CS270_vs_CS370_Comparison.md](CS270_vs_CS370_Comparison.md) for the detailed comparison.

## Topics Overview

CS 370 emphasizes AI paradigms and when to apply them, including core ideas in search, planning, probabilistic reasoning, optimization, and learning, along with ethical and societal implications. For a detailed weekly plan, see [junior-core/370/AI_Paradigms_14_Week_Course_Plan.pdf](AI_Paradigms_14_Week_Course_Plan.pdf). For ACM alignment, see [junior-core/370/CS370_ACM_Perspective.md](CS370_ACM_Perspective.md).
