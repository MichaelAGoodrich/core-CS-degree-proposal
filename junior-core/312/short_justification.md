# CS 312: Algorithms and Complexity

## Justification

CS 312 is included in the junior core curriculum as currently taught with no proposed changes. This course is already a required component of the general computer science degree and remains essential for students entering the field.

### Rationale for Inclusion

- **Foundational to CS Practice**: Algorithms and complexity analysis form the theoretical foundation for all computer science work
- **Industry Standard**: Understanding algorithm design and computational complexity is fundamental to all technical roles
- **Prerequisite Knowledge**: This course provides essential concepts referenced and applied throughout the remainder of the curriculum
- **Program Strength**: The existing course effectively serves student learning and requires no modifications

We maintain this course as-is to honor the principle of evolutionary rather than revolutionary change, recognizing that the course is already meeting its intended learning outcomes effectively.
