# CS 452 Course Topics, Learning Outcomes, and Schedule
Provided by Michael Reynolds

## Overview
There are 3 main parts:
1. SQL Mastery, Modeling, and GPT integration
2. NoSQL, Concurrency, VectorDB, System Design, Big Data, etc.
3. Final Project

## Topics and Learning Outcomes

- **SQL**  
  Become competent at querying SQL across LeetCode problems (MySQL and PostgreSQL).

- **Data Modeling**  
  Become competent at modeling data and table diagrams for a variety of modern use cases (database setup and design).

- **AI**  
  Agentic AI, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), and semantic search.

- **High Velocity Data Systems**  
  Recovery, scale-out, resilience, availability, consistent hashing, NoSQL, data change notifications, and consistent stateful distributed systems.

- **Large Data Systems**  
  Introduction to Spark, Kusto, Kafka, Data Warehousing, and ETL.

- **Data Security**  
  Theory and Row-Level Security (RLS) on Supabase.

- **Course Artifact**  
  In addition to homeworks, readings, and labs, students will complete a final project showcasing their interests and skills to potential employers.

- **Reflection and Purpose**  
  Students will reflect on why God has led them to be in this class and to learn these concepts.

- **Applications for Good**  
  Students will explore applications of class-relevant technology that could bless the world and that they could meaningfully contribute to.

- **Deep Study**  
  Students will choose an area of deep study and, with rigor and dedication, build something worth sharing.

## Required Live Interviews

You must pass **six live interviews** to pass the course:

- **4 SQL interviews**
- **2 Data Modeling & System Design interviews**

These interviews are intended to be a valuable, real-world preparation experience.

- Schedule early — you may retake interviews if needed 👍  
- **All live interviews are required** to pass the course.

## Course Plan

The goal of this course is for you to build an amazing final project that propels both your career and your efforts to make the world a better place.

Through assignments, labs, and projects, we will explore a wide range of technologies and concepts. Exams and live interview-style assessments ensure you understand key material and are prepared for real technical interviews. Finally, we hope you will network, support your classmates, and continue learning together beyond the course.
