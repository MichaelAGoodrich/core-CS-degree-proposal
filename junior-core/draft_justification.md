## Purpose

This placeholder explains how the six proposed junior-core courses advance the degree rationale in two ways:

1. **Focus on key abstractions** aligned with the program’s emphasis on durable concepts rather than transient tools.
2. **Reuse existing courses** wherever possible, with **CS 370** added to address recent developments in AI.

## How the Junior Core Advances Key Abstractions

Each junior-core course reinforces a core abstraction central to computer science:

- **CS 312 — Algorithm Design & Analysis:** algorithmic abstractions, complexity, and scalability.
- **CS 324 — Systems Programming:** systems and resource abstractions, memory, and concurrency.
- **CS 330 — Concepts of Programming Languages:** language and computation abstractions, paradigms, and semantics.
- **CS 340 — Software Design:** architectural abstractions, modularity, and maintainable systems.
- **CS 352 — Databases:** data and information abstractions, models, and query languages.
- **CS 370 — Introduction to Artificial Intelligence:** AI and decision-making abstractions relevant to modern computing practice.

Together, these courses provide a coherent abstraction-based spine that prepares students for capstone work and advanced electives.

## Use of Existing Courses (and the AI Update)

The junior core is built on established offerings to ensure stability and continuity:

- **Existing courses used:** CS 312, CS 324, CS 330, CS 340, CS 352.
- **New addition:** **CS 370** is included in response to recent developments in AI and its foundational role in contemporary computing.

This design balances curricular continuity with a targeted update where the field has evolved most rapidly.
