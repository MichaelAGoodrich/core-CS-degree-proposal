# Top 6 Prioritized Concerns

This document highlights the **six most important concerns**, based on frequency, impact, and strategic importance.

---

## 1. AI Integration in the Curriculum
- Clear gap in required AI-focused coursework
- Strong call for 1-3 required AI-integrated courses
- Emphasis on practical skills: prompting, tool use, human-AI collaboration
- Seen as critical for future relevance of the degree

---

## 2. Breadth/Depth Structure Design
- Concerns about artificial category groupings
- Too many 400-level courses
- Misalignment with student demand
- Questions about why categories and courses were chosen
- Desire for more flexible or unified elective structure

---

## 3. Implementation Details, Resource Planning, and Enrollment Viability
- Need more clarity on course content, sequencing, and ownership
- Questions about staffing, scheduling, demand balancing, and sustainable delivery
- Concern that direction is promising but implementation details are under-specified
- Concern that uneven enrollments may reduce course viability in some offerings

---

## 4. Mentoring Capacity and Quality Control in Experiential Learning
- Strong support for experiential learning, but concern about scaling mentorship
- Risk of uneven mentoring quality across pathways
- Need for consistent expectations, supervision, and assessment standards

---

## 5. Course Selection Transparency
- Lack of clarity on how core and math courses were chosen
- Need to explicitly communicate rationale and tradeoffs

---

## 6. Experiential Learning Credit Limit (3-Credit Cap)
- Concern that 3 credits may be insufficient for meaningful impact
- Suggestions included expanded credit expectations and multi-term models
- Seen as a practical limitation even by supporters of the requirement

---

## Summary
These concerns suggest a need to:
- Modernize the curriculum (AI integration)
- Improve structure and execution (electives, implementation detail, transparency, and enrollment viability)
- Strengthen experiential learning quality and scale (mentoring + adequate credit scope)
