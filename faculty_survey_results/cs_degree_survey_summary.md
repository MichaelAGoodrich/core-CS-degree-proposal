# Survey Results Summary: Proposed CS Degree

## Overview
- Total responses: 22
- Strong overall support across all components
- Highest support: Experiential learning and math foundations
- Most concern: Breadth/depth elective structure
- Alignment strongest for: Intellectual growth
- Alignment weakest for: Character building

---

## 1. Vision Statement
**Support**
- 20 support, 2 do not support

**Alignment with BYU Aims**
- Intellectually enlarging: Very strong agreement
- Character building: Mixed responses
- Lifelong learning: Generally positive

**Key Insight**
- Broad approval, but character-building alignment is less clear.

---

## 2. Junior Core
**Support**
- 19 support, 3 do not support

**Alignment**
- Strong intellectual alignment
- Moderate lifelong learning alignment
- Weaker character-building alignment

**Key Insight**
- Academically strong, but personal development outcomes may need clarification.

---

## 3. Math Foundations
**Support**
- 21 support, 1 does not support

**Alignment**
- Very strong intellectual alignment
- Mixed responses on character building and lifelong learning

**Themes from Comments**
- Strengths: Importance widely agreed upon
- Weaknesses:
  - Disagreement on specific courses
  - Desire for flexibility (e.g., alternative stats paths)
  - Questions about relevance to CS

**Key Insight**
- Agreement on importance, disagreement on implementation.

---

## 4. Breadth/Depth Electives
**Support**
- 17 support, 5 do not support

**Alignment**
- Positive overall but more neutral/disagree responses

**Themes from Comments**
- Strengths:
  - Concept of breadth + depth is supported
- Weaknesses:
  - Rigid or artificial category structure
  - Imbalance across areas
  - Hidden prerequisites
  - Need for alignment with emerging fields

**Key Insight**
- Concept is strong, implementation needs refinement.

---

## 5. Experiential Learning
**Support**
- 21 support, 1 does not support

**Alignment**
- Strong across all BYU aims

**Themes from Comments**
- Strengths:
  - Highly valuable for real-world preparation
  - Supports mentorship and interpersonal growth
  - Seen as essential and “much needed”
- Concerns:
  - Variation in mentorship quality

**Key Insight**
- Clear highlight of the proposal.

---

## Cross-Cutting Insights

### 1. BYU Aim Alignment Pattern
- Strongest: Intellectual growth
- Moderate: Lifelong learning
- Weakest: Character building

### 2. Vision vs Implementation
- Strong agreement with overall vision
- Concerns focus on specific design decisions

### 3. Priority Areas for Improvement
- Breadth/depth structure
- Math course selection and flexibility
- Clearer articulation of character-building outcomes

---

## Bottom Line
- Strong overall support for the proposal
- Key opportunity: refine implementation details while preserving the core vision
