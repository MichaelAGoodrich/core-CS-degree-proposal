# Summary of Differences: Proposed CS BS vs. Existing CS BS (2023–2024)

This table summarizes **structural and philosophical differences** between the proposed Computer Science BS degree and the existing degree requirements. It is intended as a high-level comparison for curriculum review and discussion.

---

| Dimension | Existing Degree Requirements | Proposed Degree Structure |
|---------|------------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Organizing Principle** | Course-list driven with flexible electives. | Organized around **enduring CS abstractions** (algorithms, systems, data, AI, ethics). |
| **Core Structure** | Single CS core (11 required courses). | Split into **CS Foundations** and a cohesive **Junior Core** emphasizing paradigms and integration. |
| **Junior-Level Coherence** | Junior-level courses dispersed across core and electives. | Clearly defined **Junior Core** (Algorithms, Systems, Languages, Databases, AI). |
| **AI Coverage** | AI/ML appears primarily as electives. | **AI is required** in the junior core as a foundational paradigm. |
| **Databases** | Database course is optional (elective). | **Databases required** in the junior core. |
| **Electives Structure** | Large, flexible elective pool (21 credits) with many options. | **Structured electives**: Breadth (4 areas) + Depth (1 advanced course). |
| **Breadth Requirement** | Implicit; students may cluster in one area. | **Explicit breadth across four areas** (AI/ML, HCI/Graphics, Systems/Software, Theory). |
| **Depth Requirement** | Optional and unstructured. | **Explicit depth requirement** (400-level or higher). |
| **Capstone / Experiential Learning** | Optional; fulfilled through elective options. | **Required experiential learning** (capstone, internship, or research). |
| **Capstone Placement** | Typically late-stage or optional. | **Placed after Junior Core**, before breadth/depth electives to inform choices. |
| **Math & Analytics** | Strong math requirements including physics option. | **Data- and computing-oriented math and statistics**, physics removed. |
| **Physics Requirement** | Required (PHSCS 121). | **Removed** to prioritize computing-relevant analytics. |
| **Ethics & Writing** | Ethics required; writing required. | **Ethics and writing preserved** as explicit pillar. |
| **Adaptability to Change** | Emphasizes coverage of topics and tools. | Emphasizes **abstractions and paradigms** for long-term adaptability (10–15 years). |
| **Career Preparation Model** | Preparation through coursework and optional capstone. | Preparation through **junior core integration + required experiential learning**. |
| **Exit Requirement** | Senior exit interview. | Senior exit interview. |

---

## Key Takeaway

The proposed degree shifts from a **course-accumulation model** to a **conceptual, abstraction-centered curriculum** designed for students entering a rapidly evolving computing profession with a terminal bachelor's degree.
