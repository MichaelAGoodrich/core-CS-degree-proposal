# Perspectives on AI and CS Jobs (Selected Papers)

This document provides an overview of perspectives on Artificial Intelligence and computer science jobs, synthesized from selected papers and articles. It summarizes central claims, evidence types, emphasized skills, and outlooks regarding how AI may reshape programming work and careers.

---

| Source / Author | Publication & Date | Central Claim | Evidence / Argument Type | Skills or Behaviors Emphasized | Outlook / Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Andrew Ng | LinkedIn (2024) | Coding remains essential; as AI lowers barriers, more people should learn to code. | Historical analogy (each tech shift expands coding participation). | Coding literacy, prompting, understanding AI tools. | Optimistic – AI democratizes programming and amplifies human productivity. |
| Alexander Katrompas, PhD | Medium (Jan 2024) | AI will eliminate average “day-to-day coders”; only advanced AI architects survive. | Expert testimony + economic analogy (productivity multipliers). | Theoretical CS, architecture, systems engineering, AI orchestration. | Pessimistic – drastic contraction within 3–5 years; only elite survive. |
| Timothy B. Lee | Understanding AI (Aug 2025) | Data show the worst hiring market in a decade for new programmers, coinciding with AI tool adoption. | Economic reporting, employer interviews. | AI fluency, supervision of tools, senior-level integration. | Neutral / cautious – near-term job loss, long-term uncertain. |
| Gwendolyn Rak | IEEE Spectrum (Sept 2025) | ADP payroll data confirm early-career job losses in high-AI-exposure fields like software engineering. | Quantitative evidence (Stanford Digital Economy Lab). | Adaptation to augmentation tasks; AI-critical literacy. | Evidence-driven realism – bottom of career ladder eroding. |
| Rahul Pandey (Future-Proofing) | IEEE Spectrum (Aug 2025) | Overreliance on AI tools causes shallow understanding; success depends on judgment and trust. | Practitioner reflection. | Debugging, code review, problem decomposition, accountability. | Constructive caution – develop “ownership mindset.” |
| Rahul Pandey (Learning to Code Still Matters) | IEEE Spectrum (Aug 2025) | AI multiplies productivity but expands demand for software—coding still crucial. | Economic logic (elastic demand) + historical analogy. | Computational thinking, AI-assisted design. | Strongly optimistic – AI democratizes and grows total coder base. |
| Evangelia Krassadaki & Nikolaos Matsatsinis | Handbook of Research on AI, Innovation & Entrepreneurship (2021) | AI shifts routine work to machines but creates hybrid roles combining tech and human skills. | Academic synthesis + labor-market surveys. | Math, programming, ML, plus empathy, ethics, communication. | Balanced – net transformation, not collapse. |
