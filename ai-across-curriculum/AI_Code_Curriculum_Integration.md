# Integrating AI Code Use, Evaluation, and Architecture Across the Curriculum

This document outlines a developmental strategy for integrating the responsible use, evaluation, and architecture of AI-generated code across an undergraduate Computer Science curriculum. It builds on the existing courses and proposed course structure (from the Core CS Committee), and progressively develops students' AI literacy, judgment, and architectural skills.

---

## 1. Developmental Spiral Across the Curriculum

| Year | Focus | Student’s Evolving Relationship with AI Tools |
|-----|------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Freshman | AI awareness & safe use | Students learn what AI programming tools do, their limits, and how to validate outputs. |
| Sophomore | Evaluation & disciplined application | Students quantitatively and logically evaluate AI-generated code under structured rules. |
| Junior | Deliberate design & architecture | Students integrate and supervise AI code and systems within large-scale design projects. |
| Senior / Capstone | Ownership & reflection | Students justify, document, and maintain AI-enabled systems ethically and professionally. |

---

## 2. Course-by-Course Integration Ideas

### Freshman: Intro to Programming in Python

- Introduce AI as a fallible assistant.
- Teach verification and testing habits.
- Discuss ethics, plagiarism, and attribution.
- Debug incorrect AI-generated code.

**Outcome:** Students see AI code as drafts requiring validation, not replacement.

---

### Sophomore: Data Structures in C++

- Benchmark human vs. AI-generated implementations.
- Debug AI-generated pointer and algorithm errors.
- Reflect on when AI helps or hinders.

**Outcome:** Students calibrate trust in AI empirically.

---

### Sophomore: Discrete Mathematics in Python

- Formalize AI usage constraints.
- Use AI for conjectures, not proofs.
- Critique AI reasoning and proof structure.
- Distinguish syntax from semantics.

**Outcome:** Students rigorously verify AI reasoning.

---

### Sophomore: Intro to Software Engineering (Web Development)

- Use AI for scaffolding components.
- Track AI vs. human contributions in version control.
- Peer-review AI code for security and maintainability.

**Outcome:** Students use AI responsibly within real-world architectures.

---

## 3. Junior Core Integration

### Junior: AI Paradigms

- Study foundational and modern paradigms.
- Analyze limitations of AI tools.
- Explore hybrid systems.
- Examine ethics and explainability.

**Outcome:** Students can critique AI tools at the paradigm level.

---

### Junior: Software Engineering (Advanced)

- Use AI in CI/CD pipelines.
- Evaluate AI reliability.
- Architect AI-assisted systems.

**Outcome:** Students maintain robust AI-enabled software pipelines.

---

### Junior: Databases

- Analyze how data storage affects AI performance.
- Use AI for queries and migrations.
- Evaluate data bias and integrity.

**Outcome:** Students manage data layers for AI systems.

---

### Junior: Algorithms

- Compare human-designed and AI-generated algorithms.
- Evaluate correctness and performance.

**Outcome:** Students detect flaws in AI-generated algorithms.

---

### Junior: Programming Languages

- Explore functional paradigms.
- Compare AI-generated code across paradigms.

**Outcome:** Students refactor and evaluate AI code across styles.

---

## 4. Spiral Progression of AI Competencies

| Stage | Using AI | Evaluating AI | Architecting with AI |
|-----|---------|---------------|----------------------|
| Freshman – Intro | Guided examples | Identify errors | — |
| Sophomore – Data Structures | Boilerplate | Benchmark performance | — |
| Sophomore – Discrete Math | Idea generation | Critique proofs | — |
| Sophomore – Web Dev | Templates | Security & review | Intro architectures |
| Junior – AI Paradigms | — | Model limits | Hybrid systems |
| Junior – Adv. Software Eng | Full pipeline | Reliability | System architecture |
| Capstone | Independent choice | Ethical audits | Ownership & justification |

---

## 5. Summary and Rationale

- **Freshmen** use AI critically and ethically.
- **Sophomores** evaluate AI quantitatively and logically.
- **Juniors** architect AI-assisted systems deliberately.
- **Seniors** own and justify AI-enabled designs professionally.
