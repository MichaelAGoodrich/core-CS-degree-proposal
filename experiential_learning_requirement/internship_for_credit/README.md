# Internship for Credit

This folder provides supporting documentation for the CS 199R internship-for-credit pathway.

## Building Character Through Professional Experience

Internships develop **character** in distinctive ways:

### Responsibility and Accountability
Internship work is evaluated by real supervisors with real performance expectations. Unlike coursework where effort and understanding matter more than outcomes, internships demand that students deliver reliable, maintainable code and contribute meaningfully to team goals. This accountability shapes professional integrity and work ethic.

### Collaboration and Professionalism
Internship teams are diverse mixtures of engineers, designers, and domain experts with varied communication styles and technical approaches. Students must learn to receive feedback from experienced professionals, adapt their work to team standards, and contribute as junior colleagues in authentic professional communities.

### Ethical Practice in Industry
Real-world work surfaces ethical questions absent from coursework: How do we handle security vulnerabilities? What's our responsibility to users? How do we balance technical correctness with timelines? How do we approach conflicts between management and best practices? These experiences shape students' values and ethical judgment in their field.

## Supporting Lifelong Learning

Internships prepare students for **lifelong learning** by:
- Exposing students to technologies and practices they haven't studied in courses
- Building networks of professional mentors and colleagues
- Creating evidence of learning capacity beyond coursework (real evaluation from industry supervisors)
- Establishing confidence that they can learn new domains and contribute professionally in unfamiliar contexts
