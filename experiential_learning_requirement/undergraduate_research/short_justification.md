# Undergraduate Research — Draft Justification (Under Construction)

**Status note:** This document is **under construction**. Undergraduate research currently exists as an experiential learning option (CS 497R), but the pathway requires substantial redesign to ensure it produces consistent, assessable learning outcomes comparable to internship and capstone experiences.

## Current State and Challenges

The existing undergraduate research experience operates as a relatively unstructured "free-for-all" approach that produces a **wide range of student experiences**. Key concerns include:

- **Lack of quality control**: No standardized framework ensures research experiences align with experiential learning objectives.
- **Inconsistent outcomes**: Students may engage in activities ranging from highly mentored, structured research to disconnected tasks that do not develop integrative computing skills.
- **No post-experience assessment**: There is currently no mechanism to determine whether the shared experiential learning outcomes have been satisfied.
- **Variable mentorship quality**: Without clear expectations for faculty mentors, students may not receive the guidance needed for meaningful professional and intellectual growth.

## What Needs to Be Developed

To make undergraduate research a viable experiential learning pathway, the program must be redesigned to ensure students have a **structured sequence of experiences** that produce the same kinds of learning outcomes as capstone classes and internships.

Specific design requirements include:

### 1. Structured Framework and Requirements

Faculty and student requirements should follow the pattern established in the **BYU-designed parameters for internship for credit**, including:

- Clear expectations for faculty mentors (time commitment, milestone setting, feedback frequency)
- Defined student deliverables (project proposals, progress reports, final presentations/papers)
- Regular check-ins and formative assessment
- Coordination with a program administrator (similar to the internship coordinator role)

### 2. Reflective Learning Model

The undergraduate research experience should incorporate a **reflective learning model** patterned after the approach described in Minnes, Ghanbari Serslev, and Edwards (2020). This includes:

- Structured reflection exercises that help students connect research activities to academic coursework
- Guided self-assessment of skills developed and areas for further growth
- Articulation of how research experiences inform career interests and future coursework choices
- Development of professional identity and understanding of computing's role in addressing meaningful problems

### 3. Quality Control and Assessment

To ensure consistency and accountability:

- Faculty mentors must commit to experiential learning objectives, not just research productivity
- Regular assessment of whether students are meeting shared experiential learning outcomes
- Post-experience evaluation to verify that learning outcomes were satisfied
- Mechanism for intervention if a research experience is not progressing appropriately

## Alignment with Experiential Learning Outcomes

When properly structured, undergraduate research can fulfill the shared experiential learning outcomes:

- Apply academic knowledge from the classroom to real-world problems (research questions, technical challenges).
- Articulate the value of courses in the program in preparing them for research work.
- Differentiate themselves from other students through research experience.
- Identify areas where they need further development and frame future coursework in context.
- Demonstrate the ability to learn dynamically and adapt to solve evolving problems.
- Build mentoring relationships that support future references and opportunities.
- Share real experiences and self-introspection in facing research challenges.
- Understand which aspects of computing they enjoy and want to pursue long-term.

## Next Steps

Before undergraduate research can serve as a required experiential learning pathway, the following work is needed:

1. **Design structured framework**: Develop clear guidelines, milestones, and expectations for both students and faculty mentors.
2. **Create reflective learning materials**: Adapt the reflective learning model from internship research to the research context.
3. **Establish assessment mechanisms**: Define how learning outcomes will be evaluated and verified.
4. **Pilot and refine**: Test the structured approach with a cohort of students and faculty before scaling.
5. **Secure faculty buy-in**: Ensure faculty understand the experiential learning objectives and commit to mentoring practices that support them.
