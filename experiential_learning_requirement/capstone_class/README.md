# Capstone Class

This folder outlines the capstone pathway that fulfills the experiential learning requirement through integrative, team-based projects.

## Building Character and Professional Growth

Capstone courses uniquely build **character** through several mechanisms:

### Teamwork and Communication
Team-based projects require students to articulate technical ideas, negotiate approaches, and coordinate work—building communication and leadership skills that define professional maturity. Unlike individual assignments, team dynamics surface interpersonal challenges and require strategies for resolving conflict and building trust.

### Responsibility in Real Contexts
Capstone projects are designed to produce real value—whether for campus departments, community partners, or industry clients. Students must then defend their work to stakeholders with genuine expectations and constraints, not hypothetical grading rubrics. This creates authentic responsibility where quality matters beyond a letter grade.

### Ethical Decision-Making
Real projects involve genuine tradeoffs: Should we spend engineering effort on this edge case or ship sooner? How do we balance performance with security? What's our responsibility when users misuse our system? These questions develop ethical and professional judgment in ways that textbook assignments cannot.

## Supporting Lifelong Learning

Capstone courses prepare students for **lifelong learning** by:
- Providing mentored experience in learning from ambiguous requirements and incomplete specifications
- Requiring students to research unfamiliar technologies and integrate them into solutions
- Building confidence in tackling projects larger than any single course assignment
- Establishing relationships with faculty mentors who model scholarly and professional growth
